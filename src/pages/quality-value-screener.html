How It Works

Starting from the top 500 US-listed stocks by market capitalization (minimum $5B), we apply three hard filters to remove unprofitable, extremely expensive, or heavily leveraged names. Survivors are scored on Value (40%), Quality (40%), and Safety (20%) factors, then ranked. The top 40 form the final basket.

Hard Filters

Filter 1 — P/E Ratio: 3 < P/E < 25

Excludes deeply negative-earnings or loss-making companies (P/E ≤ 3 or negative) and highly speculative growth names (P/E ≥ 25). Keeps reasonably priced earners.

Filter 2 — ROE > 0%

Ensures the company generates a positive return on shareholders' equity. Removes firms burning capital.

Filter 3 — D/E ≤ 2.0

Caps leverage. Companies with debt-to-equity above 2× carry elevated financial risk and are excluded.

Pass = (3 < P/E < 25) AND (ROE > 0) AND (D/E ≤ 2.0)

Factor Weights & Scoring

Each surviving stock is scored 0–100 on three sub-factors, then combined:

Value 40%
Earnings Yield, P/B, P/S, FCF Yield
Quality 40%
ROE, ROA, Gross Margin, Earnings Stability
Safety 20%
D/E, Interest Coverage, Current Ratio, Beta
Composite = Value × 0.40 + Quality × 0.40 + Safety × 0.20

Score Bands

Elite 80 – 100 Top-tier value + quality + safety. Strongest conviction picks.
Strong 70 – 79 Well-balanced across all three pillars. Solid candidates.
Average 60 – 69 Passed filters but weaker on one or more factors. Watch list.
Weak 0 – 59 Barely qualifies. Often only present when the screener universe is thin.

Worked Example

Re-deriving the current #1 stock from raw factor inputs:

BRK.B · Berkshire Hathaway · Rank #1 · Elite
Value   88 / 100 × 40% = 35.2
Quality   82 / 100 × 40% = 32.8
Safety   91 / 100 × 20% = 18.2
Raw weighted sum = 86.2 → Display score: 88 (linear rescaling across basket)
All three factors come from the same fundamental observation; the display score is a linear rescaling so absolute value may differ but relative ordering is preserved.

Limitations

  • Trailing fundamentals only — does not incorporate forward estimates or analyst revisions.
  • Sector-agnostic scoring may over-weight financials and industrials where P/E and D/E ranges naturally differ.
  • No momentum or technical signal — a stock can score well fundamentally while in a structural downtrend.
  • Point-in-time data from the latest quarterly filing; mid-quarter events (earnings warnings, M&A) are not captured until next refresh.

Data Sources & Freshness

Fundamentals: quarterly filings (10-Q / 10-K) via financial data APIs. Market cap and price: daily close. Refresh cadence: weekly (Sunday night EST). First snapshot: Apr 20, 2026.
