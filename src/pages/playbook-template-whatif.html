Overview in plain English

This playbook looks at every S&P 500 company that reported Q1 2026 earnings between April 1 and April 18, 2026 — 43 companies in total. We cut off at April 18 because that was the last reporting day before this page was built.

For each reporter, we compare the company's reported earnings per share against what analysts expected just before the report. We then measure how the stock moved from the last close before the report to the first close on or after the report.

How we measured the surprise

Earnings surprise = (reported EPS − consensus EPS) / |consensus EPS|, expressed as a percent. A positive surprise means the company beat; negative means it missed.

How we measured the reaction

Next-day reaction is the percentage change from the closing price just before the report to the first close on or after the report — typically the close of the next trading session.

How we grouped by sector

We used the 11 standard GICS stock-market sectors. For each sector we show how many companies reported, what share beat expectations, the typical (median) size of the beat, and the typical next-day stock reaction. Sectors with only one reporter so far (for example, Energy) are shown but reflect only that one company.
