Overview in Plain English

This strategy screens the US large-cap universe (market cap > $5B, top 500 by market cap) and keeps only companies that pass all three criteria: P/E between 3 and 25 (excluding excessively cheap or expensive), positive ROE (positive return on equity), and manageable leverage (D/E ≤ 2.0).

The remaining companies are scored using Value (cheapness, 40%) + Quality (profitability, 40%) + Safety (financial stability, 20%) to produce a composite score from 0–100. The top 40 are output with red flag annotations.

20%
valueScore = normalize( 1 / P/E ) // Lower P/E = higher score
qualityScore = normalize( ROE ) // Higher ROE = higher score
safetyScore = normalize( 1 / (1 + D/E) ) // Lower D/E = higher score
composite = 0.40 × valueScore + 0.40 × qualityScore + 0.20 × safetyScore
score = clamp(0, 100, round( 45 + 50 × (composite − min) / (max − min) ))
