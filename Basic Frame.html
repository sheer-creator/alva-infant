🖥️ Data Center (AI GPUs) Highest Narrative Heat · Blackwell Demand
Overview
Status Overview

Lead times narrowing from 52 weeks to ~28 weeks.

⚡ Blackwell Architecture GB200 NVL72 · Revenue Ramp · Supply
Overview
Status Overview

Architecture: Blackwell GPU (B100/B200/B200 Ultra) uses a 2-die design connected via NVLink-C2C at 1.8 TB/s bandwidth. The GB200 NVL72 rack packs 72 B200 GPUs and 36 Grace CPUs with 1.4 TB of HBM3e per rack.   Revenue Ramp: Blackwell went from near-zero to ~$37.5B quarterly revenue in 6 quarters — the fastest product ramp in semiconductor history. Hopper (H100/H200) revenue declining as Blackwell transitions.   Next Gen: Blackwell Ultra (GB300) sampling to hyperscalers in Q2 2026 with 50% more HBM memory. Rubin architecture (B200 successor) targeting late 2026/early 2027.

🔗 Networking InfiniBand + Spectrum-X · Second Growth Pillar
Overview
Status Overview

Product Lines: InfiniBand (scale-up AI clusters, high-bandwidth, low-latency) and Spectrum-X (scale-out Ethernet-based AI networking). Both growing strongly as AI cluster sizes expand.   Strategic Value: Networking creates full-stack lock-in — NVIDIA's CUDA + GPU + NVLink + InfiniBand/Spectrum-X forms an end-to-end moat. Mellanox acquisition (2020, $6.9B) proved highly prescient.   Competition: Arista Networks and Cisco competing on Spectrum-X/Ethernet side; InfiniBand remains NVIDIA-only. Total Networking expected to reach ~$10B annualized by end of 2026.

📉 Gross Margin Blackwell Transition Impact · Recovery Trajectory
Overview
Status Overview

Margin Pressure: Gross margins declined from 78.4% (Hopper peak, FQ1'25) to 71.3% (FQ1'26) due to Blackwell production complexity — multi-die packaging (CoWoS), NVLink switch costs, and initial yield drag.   Recovery: Margins have recovered to ~74.5% as of FQ4'26 as yields improve and Blackwell volumes scale. Management guided 74.6–75.0% for FQ1 FY2027.   Long-term Target: NVIDIA targets mid-70s gross margin normalized, with NVLink switch yield improvements and Blackwell Ultra cost reductions as key drivers. A return to 78–80% is possible by 2027 if Rubin has simpler packaging.

🌏 China / Export Controls H20 Revenue · Policy Risk · Market Shift
Overview
Status Overview

Current Status: China revenue has declined from 17% of total (FQ1'25) to ~9% (FQ4'26) due to Oct 2023 and Oct 2024 export control rules. The H20 — NVIDIA's China-specific GPU — is a downgraded B100 (NVLink disabled, bandwidth limited) currently allowed for sale.   Risk Scenario: Further restrictions on H20 could eliminate ~$4–5B of quarterly revenue (~9%). The Commerce Department has been reviewing the H20's capabilities, and a new rule is expected by mid-2026.   Mitigation: NVIDIA has designed an "H20 Lite" variant for potential tighter restrictions. Chinese cloud giants (Alibaba, Tencent, Baidu) stockpiling H20s ahead of expected restrictions.

🎮 Gaming (GeForce) RTX 5000 Launch · Stable Revenue · Low Narrative Heat
Overview
Status Overview

Market Position: GeForce RTX 5000 series (Blackwell-based) launched in January 2026, featuring the RTX 5090 (2x performance vs 4090) with 32GB GDDR7. Demand has been strong but supply-constrained due to TSMC prioritization of data center orders. Gaming remains ~6.5% of NVDA revenue.   New Angle — AI PCs: RTX 5000 series includes DLSS 4 with AI-driven multi-frame generation, positioning gaming GPUs as "AI PC" accelerators. This narrative could expand the addressable market to enterprise workstations.   Competition: AMD RX 9000 series (RDNA 4) with competitive mid-range pricing at $549 for RX 9070 XT, potentially capping NVDA's mid-range market share.
